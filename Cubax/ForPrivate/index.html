Walls - ultra-strong fiberglass concrete and energy-efficient solid polyurethane foam
Glass fiber reinforced concrete
This is concrete grade M500 reinforced in volume with alkali-resistant glass fiber, the water absorption of which is not more than 2%. Concrete with similar characteristics is used to build bridges. Such strength and water-repellent characteristics allow it to withstand aggressive climatic conditions without the need for maintenance and repair of the building for decades.
Polyurethane foam
Eco-friendly, foamed material with a closed-cell structure, the thermal insulation properties of which are 1.5 times higher than foam plastic, 2 times higher than mineral wool, 4 times higher than aerated concrete. It has high vapor permeability and does not accumulate moisture. Completely safe, does not emit harmful substances, approved for food storage. Solid polyurethane foam guarantees the complete absence of joints and seams of insulation, which increases energy efficiency and eliminates cold bridges.
The thickness of the CUBAX walls is 19 cm.
In terms of thermal insulation, this is comparable to 30 cm of mineral wool or 60 cm of aerated concrete.

How is the heating system organized?
During production, a floor heating pipe is laid in the design of each module. After installation on the site, the pipes of all modules are connected, forming a common heating system. If it is necessary to install heating radiators, pipes for them are also laid in the design during production.
